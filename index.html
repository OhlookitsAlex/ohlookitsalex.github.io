Featured Projects

Published Research on Active Noise Cancellation

Authored a physics research paper investigating and modeling Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) technology.

The paper titled, "Exploring the Effects of Frequency Composition and Timbre of Noise on Active Noise Cancellation in Over-Ear Headphones," investigated how the pitch and texture of noise affect ANC performance, tying to the technology's current real-world limitations.

It combined theoretical analysis with 72 empirical measurements to develop an adaptive Fourier Series that predicts ANC performance/characteristics based on an audio system's specific time delay; hence, depening udnerstanding and establishing a framework for future developments.

Currently, the paper is published by the Curieux Academic Journal and under revision by the Young Scientist Journal.

Project Audio Justice Initiative (AJI)

An award winning creativity and service project intended to bring more great audio quality to consumer audio.

Stemming from UNSDG 9 and the mass misrepresentation in consumer audio, I started the AJI to provide genuinly good audio as accessibly as possible.

Specifically, the AJI work to improve the tuning of popular headphones/speakers by developing specific equalizer settings, a tool to adjust the relative loudness of different frequencies. These settings are then shared online through platforms of large impacts such as Reddit and other other forums.

So far, the AJI has worked on 5 bluetooth speakers, 4 pairs of headphones, has been acknowledged by Anker and Rtings.com, won 1st place the Renaissance 2024 CAS Project Fair, and is still on-going!
